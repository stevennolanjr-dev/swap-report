The Through-Line

Today is a study in negotiating by fire. Three live diplomatic tracks are open at once: the NATO summit in Ankara, the US-Iran talks over the strait, and the Trump-Zelensky effort to pressure Russia toward a settlement. On the eve of each, the relevant party reached for ordnance rather than restraint. Iran resumed strikes in Hormuz to keep its leverage physical while its negotiators talk. Ukraine put a fire in Russia's largest refinery 2,500 kilometers away to arrive at the summit with a demonstration that the war's cost now reaches Siberia. Russia emptied a barrage over Kyiv to make sure the alliance opens its meeting under Moscow's shadow. This is Clausewitz's oldest point rendered in a single news cycle: force is not the alternative to bargaining but its continuation, and the two run together rather than in sequence. What each actor bought was position at a table that has not yet been set, and the violence is the argument they intend to make once they sit down. The through-line is that nobody in this week's diplomacy is choosing between talking and shooting; they are doing both, on purpose, and the shooting is the part meant to be believed.

How It Aged

Aged Poorly Monday's brief led with the argument that the Strait of Hormuz "has stopped being a military story and become an economic one," that the shooting had stopped and only the risk premium remained. Within 24 hours Iran resumed missile strikes on shipping in the strait, setting a tanker ablaze off Oman and hitting a Qatari gas carrier. Verdict: the call was overtaken by events, and honesty requires saying so plainly. The economic-aftershock framing mistook a one-week political truce for a de-escalation, and treated residual risk as the whole story when the active threat had merely paused. Lesson: a chokepoint held open by an expiring truce is not a chokepoint that has been demilitarized, and a party still holding the card has every incentive to remind the room it is real while the talks are live. The stranded-crews and shuttle-run economics were correct as far as they went; the error was calling the kinetic phase over when it was only between rounds.
