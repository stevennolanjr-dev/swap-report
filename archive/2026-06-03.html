Executive Actions
Federal Register  |  White House  |  CNN  |  The Hill
EO Trump signs vaccine schedule EO directing CDC to shrink core childhood recommendations
President Trump signed an executive order directing CDC and its Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices to review an HHS scientific assessment and update the US childhood vaccine schedule. The assessment found the US recommends more doses than any peer nation, identified 11 diseases as the consensus core, and moved vaccines for flu, hepatitis A and B, rotavirus, some meningitis variants, and RSV to shared decision-making or high-risk-only status. The EO publishes in today's Federal Register. Implementation timelines depend on ACIP review.
Trump EO directs CDC to shrink the core childhood vaccine schedule toward a peer-country standard. 11 consensus diseases remain core; flu, hepatitis, RSV shift to shared decision-making. Significant policy shift pending ACIP review.
Federal Register  |  FL Voice News
EO Federal lands EO rescinds Nixon-era off-road vehicle restrictions from 1972 and 1977
A second executive order signed Friday and published in today's Federal Register rescinds Executive Orders 11644 (1972) and 11989 (1977), which restricted off-road vehicle use on public lands. The administration called the orders outdated and counterproductive. The action opens federal lands more broadly to OHV use and is consistent with the broader deregulatory direction on land access.
Two Nixon-era off-road vehicle EOs rescinded. Opens public lands to broader OHV use. Lower controversy than the vaccine EO; both published the same day.
