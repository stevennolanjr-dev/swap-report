Through-line

The Beijing summit is functioning as a clearing house for unresolved files. China's SAM commitment to Iran is the appetizer, structured to give Trump a deliverable his domestic audience reads as a win while costing Beijing very little; the surface-to-air transfer was never the most likely Chinese arms package to Iran in the first place. The harder files remain unresolved: Taiwan language, chip export controls, the basic terms of trade. On the war files, the structural facts hold. Hormuz is closed; Aramco's 100 million barrels per week is a published number that any market reads as durable; Ukrainian aid is now hostage to the Yermak case as much as to Russian operations; the Cuba option is being negotiated inside the Senate Republican conference because the administration cannot get it past committee leadership without that conversation. The Capito and Ricketts wins close the question of whether the GOP base will primary out incumbents over the war supplemental. The base is not where the open question sits. It sits in the Senate, in Brussels, and on the tape.
