Through-line

Yesterday's brief argued the cordon-and-escort framework had load-tested cleanly at the rotary-wing rung and the next test would be at a higher rung. Instead the framework moved sideways: Washington paused the escort mission to give the Pakistan channel room to deliver text. Three threads converge in that decision. Pakistan's mediation has produced something with a shape (the public PM thank-you would not have been issued without a draft on the table). Beijing has formalized the broker role at FM level, which raises the diplomatic cost of collapse for both sides. And the long-form analysis on the desk this morning is uniformly skeptical: a forever-war framing in Foreign Policy, a capability-gaps framing in War on the Rocks, and a Jerusalem Post intelligence read that the air campaign did not move the nuclear breakout timeline. The optimism in the morning headlines and the skepticism in the analysis are pointing at the same fact pattern: the parties have stopped fighting at the warning-shot level and started writing on a piece of paper neither one wants to sign yet.
