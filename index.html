USS Spruance Intercepts Iranian Ship Attempting to Breach Naval Blockade, CENTCOM Says STRIPES

The Spruance — a Flight IIA Arleigh Burke destroyer operating under CENTCOM — fired warning shots, disabled the Touska’s propulsion, and boarded. The ship is now en route to a U.S.-controlled port for adjudication. Fifth Fleet has shifted its public language from “freedom of navigation” to “blockade enforcement” in less than a week.

SWAP's Take

Saturday’s seizure is the rung that yesterday’s report said planners least want to pull, pulled. The historical rhyme is the 1987–88 Tanker War, where escalation was eventually managed — but only after the Vincennes shot down Iran Air 655 by mistake. The information environment today is hostile to that kind of recovery. The Touska boarding was filmed from Iranian shore batteries, IRGC UAVs, commercial satellite, and at least one civilian ship in the area; the narrative window for shaping legitimacy closed in hours. The harder question is what the off-ramp looks like. Iran now has to either absorb the seizure publicly or retaliate against something U.S.-flagged. There is no third option that preserves regime credibility at home. That is the definition of a rung with no next lower rung — which is why the seizure isn’t a pressure tool, it’s an escalation. Watch for IRGC action against allied-flagged tankers, not U.S.-flagged. That’s the face-saving version of retaliation, and it is what the planners should be positioning against.

North Korean Leader Oversees Latest Test of Ballistic Missiles with Cluster Warheads STRIPES · REUTERS

KCNA released imagery showing Kim and his daughter at an observation post. ROK and U.S. tracking assessed the cluster dispersion pattern as intentional — this is a warhead-mode test, not a range test. Seventh missile event in 23 days. The cadence is now unambiguous.

SWAP's Take

Cluster warheads change the defensive math. A terminal-phase interceptor — PAC-3, THAAD, Aegis BMD — is optimized against a single reentry body. Cluster submunitions dispersing before intercept window mean either a much larger interceptor salvo per inbound round, or accepting higher leakage. For ROK airbase operations the scenario is specific: suppress a dispersal airfield long enough to deny sortie generation in the first 48 hours of a conflict. The protracted-war logic applies — Pyongyang is not building for a war they expect to fight next month; they are building a credible counter-airfield threat that makes regional allies hesitate to concentrate forces in the first place. Pair the cluster-warhead capability with the launch cadence and the shape of the force is visible: Kim is building a menu of denial options, not a single decisive capability. The intercept problem is harder than it was six months ago.
