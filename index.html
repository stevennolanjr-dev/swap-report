Through-line

The 2026 NDS pivot to Western Hemisphere security plus Golden Dome was supposed to free bandwidth for Pacific deterrence and homeland defense. Three months into operationalization, the U.S. Navy is escorting commercial ships through Hormuz at $700 million-plus per day, the IRGC is claiming damage to U.S. destroyers, and ROK and Japan are calibrating nuclear options because alliance neglect feels structural. The strategy and the operational tempo are pointing in opposite directions. The EPRS read of the NDS as overstretch-prone, the CIA assessment of three to four months of Iranian endurance, the Cha-Govella warning on East Asia nuclearization, and the Chatham House quantification of Taiwan-blockade economics all triangulate the same gap. The Iran war is not the strategic choice the NDS was written to support; it is the operational reality the NDS will be revised against.
