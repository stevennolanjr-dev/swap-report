Through-Line
The through-line this Saturday is institutional friction at multiple levels of the U.S. government. The Pentagon is simultaneously resisting a federal court on HIV enlistment, placing a convicted insurrectionist in a sensitive national security role, banning journalists from its press office, and defying a separate ruling on the Kennedy Center. The Iran war's ceasefire holds nominally but not operationally. None of these events individually represents a break; together they suggest an administration treating courts, press norms, and personnel vetting as negotiable constraints rather than fixed limits. The Friday market selloff, driven by chip-stock disappointment and a hot jobs print, reinforces the fragility of the soft-landing narrative. The question entering next week: does the Iran back-channel produce something signable, or does sporadic shooting become the new baseline?
