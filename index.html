Through-Line
The ceasefire negotiation with Iran and the drone incident in Romania share a structural feature: both are situations where adversaries are probing how much ambiguity the United States and its allies will absorb before triggering a harder response. Tehran is testing whether the MOU framework can be strung along without conceding the nuclear file. Moscow is testing whether NATO's airspace is defensible when direct confrontation is politically inconvenient. In both cases, the answer so far has been "more than you might expect." That is not necessarily the wrong call, but it is a posture that accumulates risk. The depleted stockpile report adds a third data point: the capacity to escalate, if Washington chose to, is constrained in ways it was not two years ago. These three signals are not unrelated.
