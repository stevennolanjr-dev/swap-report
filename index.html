Through-line

When Iran calls the U.S. proposal "surrender" and the U.S. calls Iran's response "totally unacceptable," the gap between the two positions is not being closed by diplomacy, it is being held open by both sides for their own domestic audiences. The 20-warship blockade is Washington's answer. Tehran's answer is a counteroffer built around reparations, which is a position the U.S. political system cannot accept. The gap is not a negotiating gap; it is a commitment gap, and commitment gaps close through force, exhaustion, or a third party with enough leverage to compel both sides toward a number they can both live with. That third party is sitting down with Trump in Beijing today, and it has every incentive to watch the gap stay open for another week before deciding whether to be useful.

The restitution case was completed; the portrait will be returned to the original family's heirs. Approximately 650,000 objects remain unresolved in European provenance inventories eight decades after the war. The art finds its way home, one piece at a time, across timelines longer than any of the people involved in the original crime lived to see.
Philippine VP Sara Duterte impeached for the second timeINDO-PACIFIC
BBC · May 10–11, 2026
The Philippine House of Representatives has impeached Vice President Sara Duterte for a second time, sending the case to the Senate for trial. The first impeachment was dismissed on procedural grounds; this one cleared the House with a larger majority. The case turns on allegations of misuse of government funds and threats against President Marcos. The episode is a stress test for Philippine democratic institutions at a moment when both the U.S. and China are competing for Manila's alignment. How the Senate trial runs will be watched closely in both Washington and Beijing.
