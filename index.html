Bottom Line Up Front
  • Iran blockade lifted, deal unsigned: Trump announced the naval blockade on Iran is lifted, but no formal ceasefire agreement has been signed. Rubio is briefing Congress; Tehran is calling it a betrayal of diplomacy while separately acknowledging talks continue.
  • Americans wounded in Kuwait: Iranian strikes hit Kuwait; several U.S. personnel were injured. The incident complicates the deal timeline and raises alliance cohesion questions across the Gulf.
  • Hegseth at Shangri-La: The Defense secretary framed Indo-Pacific posture around "quiet" capability-building, softened public rhetoric on China post-Xi summit, and pressed allies on defense spending. MQ-28 Ghost Bat confirmed flying from a U.S. Navy base in the Pacific.
  • Three EOs signed: The President signed executive orders adjusting aluminum, steel, and copper tariff regimes; realigning childhood vaccine recommendations; and removing restrictions on access to federal lands. All three elevated in the Federal Register this morning.
  • Russian drone hits Romania: A Russian drone struck an apartment building in Romania, injuring civilians. NATO vowed to defend alliance territory; Romania deployed F-16s. The first confirmed strike on NATO civil infrastructure this conflict cycle.
Through-Line
The Iran situation is in the most dangerous phase of the negotiation, not the most dangerous phase of the war. Lifting the blockade without a signed deal removes the primary U.S. leverage instrument while the core sticking points (nuclear enrichment, assets, Hormuz access guarantees) remain unresolved. Tehran reads concession without signature as weakness; the Republican hawks read it the same way. Hegseth's performance at Shangri-La was calibrated to hold the Indo-Pacific posture while Washington's attention drifts toward the Gulf deal. The Romania drone strike is a separate signal: Moscow is stress-testing NATO's Article 5 threshold on civilian infrastructure, and the response so far is F-16 deployments and strong language, not collective action. These three threads (Gulf deal, Indo-Pacific signaling, European threshold-testing) are not unrelated. They are the simultaneous pressure campaign.
How It Aged

Hold May 30 call: "No deal announced after Trump's 'final determination' meeting." Five days later the blockade is lifted unilaterally and the deal is still unsigned. The read that Trump would not close on Iranian terms without more movement on enrichment is holding; the blockade lift without signature was the more dovish outcome than assessed.
